## IV. USD1: Why Model Calls Use Stablecoin Settlement

USD1 is WLFI's dollar stablecoin. WLFI says USD1 redeems 1:1 for USD, backed by dollars, US government money market funds, and cash equivalents; monthly reserve reports and PoR links provided.
In WorldClaw's narrative, USD1 is settlement asset. Model calls bill per token; packages buyable in USD1; future agent payments may center on USD1.
So WorldRouter's "~30% off gateway" is not just pricing—it is a USD1 use case: buy credits, track consumption; future agent-to-agent calls and on-chain settlement may continue in USD1.
## V. AgentPay SDK: Agents Pay—But Not Freely

![](https://mmbiz.qpic.cn/sz_mmbiz_png/ibu320hOC23J7FJr2qpl8zpyJH3cClCYIFI3icNPWdA3ff5YyaL4LYfNGT80Y1LGWic8FNnRs7B90pWU7bT6jVwsricfZY3IYgKgPvaxSt0yPyM/0?from=appmsg)
Diagram: AgentPay SDK emphasizes policy constraints, human approval, local signing, on-chain USD1 settlement.
AgentPay SDK is WLFI's open toolkit for AI agents to hold, move, and manage USD1 under policy constraints—moving "AI agent payments" from concept to developer tools.
- **Self-hosted locally**: wallet keys generated/stored on operator machine; WLFI does not custody wallets or access key material.
- **Policy before signature**: every request passes spend policy (limits, address allowlists, thresholds) before signing.
- operations above thresholds can pause for review.
- **Local signing, no telemetry**: docs say signing is local; SDK sends no telemetry to WLFI or third parties.
- docs mention skill packs for Claude Code, Codex, OpenClaw, etc.
## VI. Why WLFI Enters the AI Agent Track

WorldRouter "~30% off" alone looks like cost optimization; add USD1 and AgentPay SDK and it becomes infrastructure narrative.

Stablecoins need usage, not just issuance. Model calls, agent service purchases, agent-to-agent settlement are high-frequency potential scenes.
### 2. Embed finance in AI workflows

DeFi is often low-frequency; AI/agent workflows may be high-frequency. WorldRouter + USD1 embeds financial infrastructure in daily dev/user toolchains.
### 3. Capture the agent economy payment layer

If agents need APIs, data, services, micropayments—whoever provides payment policy, permissions, audit may become agent-economy infrastructure.
