Story
Origin and background: from consortium vision to global infrastructure
Canton Network launched officially in May 2023, developed by Digital Asset and driven by a consortium of banks, exchanges, and technology giants. Founding members included BNP Paribas, Capgemini, CBOE, Deloitte, Deutsche Börse, and 30+ institutions—aiming to solve data silos and privacy leaks in financial markets and enable "Synchronized Finance." Unlike general-purpose public chains such as Ethereum, Canton was positioned from birth as a "privacy-first open blockchain" tailored for institutional use, balancing decentralized transparency with regulatory privacy needs.
Its core philosophy stems from Digital Asset's Daml smart-contract platform, used as early as 2018 in J.P. Morgan's Onyx system. Canton Network's launch marked the formal opening of capital-markets blockchainization, expected to drive trillions of dollars on-chain and usher in a "real-economy tokenization" era. By September 2025 the ecosystem had grown to nearly 400 participants spanning leading global financial institutions and technology providers.

Governance structure: transparent collaboration via the Global Synchronizer Foundation
Canton Network is governed by the Global Synchronizer Foundation (GSF), established July 2024 and managed by the Linux Foundation—ensuring independence, transparency, and sustainability. GSF, a nonprofit, coordinates Super Validator operations and voting, maintains best practices, and drives the open-source Splice project including Canton Coin tokenomics.
GSF membership is tiered for fair participation:
- **Premier Members**: Five North Digital, 7Ridge, Broadridge, Cumberland, Digital Asset, Euroclear, and 10+ core institutions providing strategic guidance.
- **General Members**: Chainlink Labs, Copper, Kaiko, Moody's, and 30+ startups and technology firms focused on innovation.
- **Associate Members**: Free for governments and nonprofits such as HKMA and GBBC, subject to board approval.
In March 2025 Goldman Sachs, Moody's, and HKMA joined GSF, strengthening global influence. This multi-tier model decentralizes decisions while meeting GDPR and other regulatory requirements, avoiding single-entity dominance.

Core technology: privacy and interoperability as dual drivers
Canton Network is a Layer-1 smart-contract blockchain with a unique two-tier consensus mechanism enabling unlimited horizontal scaling and network-wide interoperability. Its stack centers on Daml smart contracts plus the Canton protocol for privacy-preserving efficient synchronization.
- **Daml smart contracts and privacy**: Daml is an open-source smart-contract language for multi-party distributed applications. It defines visibility and authorization rules at the contract layer—who may view or modify specific contract content. The Canton protocol enforces these rules via end-to-end encryption and need-to-know principles—transaction data visible only to relevant parties; unauthorized nodes cannot access full content. This solves public-blockchain "over-transparency," supporting data minimization and right-to-be-forgotten, aligning with GDPR and financial regulation. Privacy is highly configurable: sub-transaction privacy at the smart-contract level maintains ledger integrity even with malicious nodes present.
- **Global Synchronizer**: The network "heart"—a decentralized synchronization service routing messages via sync domains, providing consistent transaction ordering and commit confirmation. Nodes may connect multiple sync domains (centralized or decentralized implementations), breaking application silos. Native token **Canton Coin (CC)** pays transaction fees and incentivizes validators.
- **Interoperability and scalability**: Canton forms a "network of networks"—Daml applications interact seamlessly; assets transfer across nodes and record on multiple ledgers, forming a virtual global ledger. Horizontal scaling is unbounded, supporting high-throughput financial transactions such as derivatives settlement.
These technologies make Canton Network an institutional blockchain benchmark—throughput at arbitrary scale while preserving privacy and compliance.

Development trajectory and latest news: from pilots to mainstream adoption
Canton Network grew rapidly, entering high-growth in 2025. April 2025's *A Technical Primer* detailed privacy configurations, expanding the developer community. In June Digital Asset closed $135M led by DRW Venture Capital and Tradeweb Markets, with Goldman Sachs and Citadel Securities participating, accelerating institutional and DeFi adoption. Kaiko was approved as a Super Validator, supplying digital-asset market data.
In July four liquidity providers (B2C2, Cumberland DRW, FalconX, GSR) joined an on-chain collateral management program developing tokenized bilateral crypto-derivatives solutions. In August Digital Asset and an industry working group completed a landmark pilot: on-chain US Treasury repo trades on Canton Network—marking 24/7 blockchainization of the repo market.
September brought highlights: BNP Paribas and HSBC joined as privacy-centric blockchain participants (Sep 9); an official blog on on-chain collateral for bilateral crypto derivatives explored privatized stablecoin payments on public chains (Sep 10); BMCP became a node operator strengthening European asset services (Sep 11). CBTC (Bitcoin-backed token) launched on Canton, advancing institutional Bitcoin adoption. The Asia Stablecoin Conference (Sep 25), sponsored by Canton, will focus on RWA interoperability.
These milestones reflect Canton Network's leap from 2023 pilots to 2025 ecosystem explosion—400+ participants.

Application scenarios: end-to-end capital-markets optimization
Canton Network's core value lies in financial applications where privacy enables sensitive collaboration:
- **Post-trade capital markets**: Pilots enable asset transfer and real-time synchronization, optimizing clearing and settlement—validated by Goldman Sachs and BNP Paribas under regulatory compliance.
- **RWA tokenization**: On-chain representation of Treasuries, derivatives, and Bitcoin—August repo pilot and July collateral management handling trillions-scale assets.
- **Payments and stablecoins**: Private stablecoin payments combined with public-blockchain infrastructure for cross-border settlement; HKMA participation explores Asian market potential.
- **Institutional crypto**: Integrations with Chainlink and Copper support DeFi bridging with reduced risk.
These scenarios improve efficiency (lower intermediary costs) while enhancing transparency and auditability.
