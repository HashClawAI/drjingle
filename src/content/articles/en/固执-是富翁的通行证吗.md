## Body

2010 Tesla board: could Model S's touchscreen go back to physical buttons? Musk reportedly: "Customers will adapt."
Same era Apple: Jobs stared at a prototype, scrapped a finalized UI. Colleagues recalled: not a "discussion"—a "notification."
Read enough biographies and documentaries and you conclude: **the more stubborn, the more successful.** Musk, Jobs, Bezos, Jensen Huang—a roster that looks like a pass for "ignore advice to win."
But the question is backwards.

---

### I. The "Stubbornness" You See May Be Three Different Things

Everyday Chinese bundles at least three traits as "stubborn":
| Surface behavior | Essence | Link to wealth |
|------------------|---------|----------------|
| Ignoring boards, overturning plans | **Vision anchoring** + information filtering | Sometimes necessary in innovation; disaster in mature industries |
| Ten years on one thing | **Delayed gratification** + compounding | Correlates with conscientiousness—a stabler predictor |
| Public fights, never apologizing | **Antagonistic personality** + media strategy | No stable causal link to wealth—mostly style |
Angela Duckworth's 2007 **grit**—passion and persistence for long goals—correlates with achievement in studies. But grit ≠ deafness: it's **sustained investment in the right thing**, not **death grip on the wrong one**.
Big Five meta-analyses: high achievers correlate more stably with **conscientiousness** than with low agreeableness. Lower agreeableness—more conflict, less harmony—shows up in founder samples, but that ≠ "must be stubborn."
**Reverse the causality:** not stubborn → success, but choose an extremely hard path → must absorb massive rejection → narrated afterward as stubborn. Psychology calls this **survivorship bias**: you see Musk who survived; not the 999 equally stubborn bankruptcies.

---

### II. Forbes 2026 Top Ten: Stubbornness Isn't Standard—Conviction Is

Forbes June 2026 live list (daily price swings)—top ten mostly US tech. Rough personality mapping from public bios and interviews:
**Musk (★★★★★)** First principles + antagonistic conviction: "Physics is the only rule."
**Page / Brin (★★★☆☆)** Quiet radicalism: X Lab, Calico bets, less public fighting.
**Bezos (★★★★☆)** **Vision stubborn, detail flexible**—Day 1 culture; AWS from internal tool to trillion business.
**Ellison (★★★★☆)** Competition-driven toughness: litigation, M&A, cloud pivot.
**Dell (★★★☆☆)** Operational grit: take-private restructuring—structural stubborn, tactical flexible.
**Zuckerberg (★★★★☆)** Product conviction + social calculus: Instagram/WhatsApp, metaverse rebrand—looks stubborn when wrong.
**Jensen Huang (★★★★☆)** 30-year single bet: CUDA all-in from 2006 when almost nobody believed in general-purpose GPU compute.
**Arnault (★★★☆☆)** Patient empire builder: 40 years luxury M&A—not flashy, extremely persistent on targets.
**Ballmer (★★☆☆☆)** Outlier: high-energy executor, sales grit—not vision stubborn—proves "non-stubborn CEO + right time/platform" can reach top ten.
Patterns:
1. **9/10 tech/platform wealth**—edge is **conviction** on tech or trends, not generic stubbornness.
2. **No Buffett type in top ten**—gentle, patient, anti-stubborn can still be mega-rich; recent list heads skew high-beta tech.
3. **Page / Arnault prove: low antagonism ≠ low achievement**; stubborn can be silent.

---

### III. Non-Consensus Premium: More Valuable Than "Stubborn"

Howard Marks in *The Most Important Thing*: **"To achieve above-average returns, you must do something different—and be right."** That's **contrarian correctness**—looks stubborn externally; core is **independent judgment + tolerance for isolation**.
Bezos's variant: **Strong opinions, weakly held**—hold vision, update on evidence.
Musk on reusable rockets resembles the former; some social product calls resemble lacking the latter—**same trait, different domains, opposite outcomes**.
Jobs never long held global #1 wealth, but narrative influence exceeded rank. Isaacson + org behavior consensus: **Jobs's product conviction was asset, interpersonal rigidity liability**—getting fired from Apple was partly stubbornness's price.

---

### IV. If You Imitate, Which Layer?

Better models:
- vision fixed, tactics fluid
- single belief for 20 years
- slow is fast—M&A integration patience
Not Musk fighting on X, or Jobs berating engineers—those are **side effects**, not the formula.
Research: **grit and conscientiousness** predict success better than "tough personality"—and the list heavily filters **era + asset prices** luck. 2026 top ten mostly AI beta beneficiaries: NVIDIA, xAI, Oracle cloud—**stubbornness without the right cycle is expensive personality theater.**

---

### Closing

World's top ten are mostly **terrifyingly persistent on one thing**—but stubbornness isn't the pass.
**Persisting long enough on the right thing** is.
