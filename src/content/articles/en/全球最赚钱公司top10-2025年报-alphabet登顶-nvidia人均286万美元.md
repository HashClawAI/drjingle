## Body

> FY2025: Alphabet ~$132.2B net profit leads; NVIDIA $120.1B total, ~$2.86M/employee; Tether and Jane Street per-capita still far above listed giants.

"Who is the world's most profitable company?" sounds simple—the answer depends on **what you're measuring**.

By **FY2025 total net profit**, Alphabet leads at ~$132.2B, followed by NVIDIA, Apple, Microsoft; Saudi Aramco remains the top non-tech giant. By **profit per employee**, Tether and Jane Street push figures to tens of millions; among listed names, NVIDIA leads at ~$2.86M/person.

Based on **FY2025 annual reports and official attestation reports**, two TOP 10 lists with comparison charts. Research reference only—not investment advice.

## I. Methodology: Why "FY2025" Has Different Fiscal Calendars

**Total list**: latest full-fiscal-year net profit, USD-converted. Alphabet through Dec 31, 2025; Apple through Sep 2025; NVIDIA through Jan 25, 2026; Microsoft through Jun 2025—not the same calendar window, common internationally.

**Per-capita list**: net profit ÷ year-end employees. Private firms have limited disclosure; Jane Street reports sometimes use adjusted EBITDA vs. net income—noted separately.

**Reading note:** Fortune Global 500 (2025) converts Aramco to ~$105B; this article uses Aramco's riyal report (~$93.4B) vs Alphabet ($132.2B). Rankings shift with methodology; "tech + energy + finance" structure holds.

## IV. Tether and Jane Street: Private Per-Head Ceilings

Including privates requires a separate table:

**Tether (USDT issuer)**
2025 attestation: **net profit over $10B** (down from ~$13B in 2024, still elevated). ~300 employees → **~$33.3M net profit per head** (rough). Profit mainly from reserve investments (Treasuries etc.)—not a headcount game.

**Jane Street (quant market maker)**
2025 trading revenue ~$39.6B, adjusted EBITDA ~$31.2B; ~3,500 staff → **~$8.91M/head on EBITDA** (not GAAP net income—mind the metric). 2025 comp pool ~$9.4B, doubled from 2024.

**Citadel Securities** and peers sit in the same tier—far above traditional banks. Renaissance, Jump barely disclose—no precise ranking.

## V. Why Total and Per-Capita Are Different Answers

**Total**: scale × margin—Alphabet, NVIDIA lead; Big Tech + Aramco + TSMC form tier one.

**Per-capita**: profit ÷ headcount—stablecoins, quant finance, high-margin chips, toll-booth exchanges often win.

**Three traps when reading lists:**
① Net profit TOP 10 ≠ "best stocks to buy";
② Per-capita TOP 10 ≠ "average employee compensation";
③ Tether per-head figures don't extrapolate—regulation, rates, crypto cycles, and hiring quickly rewrite conclusions.
